# Quote Concession Playbook (Internal)

INTERNAL ONLY. Not customer-facing. Never published to the site, the
catalog, or email as a policy. This lives only in how a quote is built.
Standing policy until win/loss data shows price is the real loss reason.

## Posture

Value-led. There is no published educator discount and no fixed tier
model. The catalog is price-free; price resolves on the quote. We compete
on terms, service, curation, and ethics. Pricing stays discretionary and
unpublished. Decide on margin, not on a percentage.

## What a rep may grant without sign-off

Value and terms first. These cost less than margin and matter most to
school procurement:

- Free consolidated multi-classroom shipping; free or upgraded freight on
  a large PO.
- Sole-source letters, W-9 / COI turnaround, a standards-mapping sheet.
- A dedicated contact and priority stock allocation for the order.
- Loaner or sample of a hero piece, credited if they order.
- Seamless tax-exempt handling.
- Hold the current quarter's price for fall fulfillment (quote validity
  as the concession).
- A small first-order goodwill add-on (for example a felt mat or a gnome
  set) to land a new district. One time, not repeated on reorders.

## What needs owner sign-off

- Any price reduction on the order beyond a small first-order courtesy.
- Order-size price moves: nothing under about $2,000; a small concession
  on $2,000 to $10,000; a negotiated number above $10,000.
- Competitive match: only the items that overlap a comparable quote
  (Lakeshore, Kaplan, Amazon Business), never the whole catalog.
- Bundle priced below the sum of its parts.
- Net 60 (per the payment-terms ladder) granted in place of a price cut.
- Split shipment or split billing for a fiscal-year or grant window.

## How to log it

On the draft order, record the reason in plain words: "matched competing
quote," "new-district goodwill," "volume concession, $14k PO," "Net 60
approved in lieu of discount." This is how we learn whether price is
actually losing deals.

## Guardrails

- Never written as a standing policy or shown on the site or catalog.
- Lead with value and terms; touch price only on large or competitive
  deals, case by case.
- Rides the existing staff-mediated draft-order flow. No system change.
