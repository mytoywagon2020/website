The world of fairy play

A fairy village is small enough to carry and large enough to live inside for an afternoon.

Felt homes, little wooden folk, and soft play mats gather into one world a young child sets up, changes, and returns to. Nothing in a fairy village asks to be finished, because the tending itself is the play.

A fairy village invites children into unhurried, self-directed narrative, exactly the kind of open, imaginative space that educators know fosters the deepest learning. There are no batteries, no instructions, and no single right way to play.
