The Ecosystem

A fairy village is a small, gatherable world. It is built from felt homes, little wooden folk, and the loose parts a child brings to it, and it can be arranged, taken apart, and returned to again.

This ecosystem is best for Pre-K to Kindergarten, where small-world play carries so much of a child's early storytelling and sense of order. It belongs on a nature table or in a Reggio-inspired piazza, where children can revisit it across the week and watch it change with the season.

The pieces are open-ended on purpose. There is no single right village, only the one a child makes and remakes, which is exactly the kind of play that makes a small world feel like her own.
