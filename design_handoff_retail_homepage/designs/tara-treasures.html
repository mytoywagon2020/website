Tara Treasures.

Designed in Melbourne, hand-felted in Nepal by 350+ women artisans. The first toy brand in Australia to gain Fair Trade Certification, and the maker behind a quarter of the felt on our shelves.

A founder story

Jag & Jooli, after the earthquake.

Tara Treasures began as a Melbourne-market side hustle. Jag Gurung grew up in a village in the mountains of Nepal; Jooli Chan grew up in Melbourne with a designer's eye and a love of children's craft. They met, married, and started selling handmade Nepalese goods at local markets on weekends.

The 2015 Nepal earthquake changed the scale of the work. Watching the devastation in Jag's home country, they made the decision to go back, formally — not as charity, but to build sustainable employment for women in the communities that had raised him.

"We were united by a shared passion to make a positive impact on the world."Jag Gurung and Jooli Chan, founders

The name

Tara, goddess of compassion.

"Tara" is the name of the Buddhist goddess of compassion and peace. The studio is named for her, intentionally.

The work reflects it: women working from home or in small shared studios near their families, fair wages, sustainable materials, and toys made slowly enough to outlast a child's first decade. Compassion is the production model, not the marketing.

How it is made

One piece, by two hands.

  • Wool. 100% GOTS-certified New Zealand merino, sourced direct from the South Island. Hand-carded in Kathmandu.
  • Dyes. Azo-free, non-toxic, child-safe. No heavy metals, no synthetic colorants.
  • Felting. Wet-felted by hand. No machinery. Each piece takes 4 to 24 hours depending on size.
  • Stitching. Hand-stitched by the same artisan from start to finish. Each piece is signed by the woman who made it.
  • Working conditions. Women set their own hours, work from home or in shared studios near their families. Fair wages guaranteed, health stipends, no piecework pressure.
  • Packaging. Recycled and recyclable, designed to minimize waste.
