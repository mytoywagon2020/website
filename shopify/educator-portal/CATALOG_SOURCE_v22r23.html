Why Natural Materials Matter3
The Sensory Shift
Why Felt & Wood
Belong in Every Classroom

As classrooms move toward mindful learning, natural materials are reclaiming their rightful place at the heart of early childhood education.

Wool warms in the hand. Wood holds the weight of the tree it came from. Children notice these qualities, even when they can’t name them, and that sensory depth helps them settle, focus, and stay with a task long enough to think it through.

Natural materials are open-ended by design: no instructions, no single right answer. That openness is where creativity, problem-solving, and executive function take root. The skills children build through this kind of play extend well beyond early childhood.

Why We Started

My Toy Wagon began in 2020 with a simple conviction, that children deserve materials made with the same care and intention as the adults who teach them. Not plastic that breaks in a week. Not novelty that bores in an afternoon. Something real, something lasting, something worth handing down.

Every product we carry has been handled, opened, and played with. We carry it because it earns its place: in the classroom, on the shelf, in the hands of a child who does not know yet that she is learning.

What We Believe

We believe open-ended play is not a curriculum style; it is a fundamental respect for the child. A piece of natural wood, a handful of wool, a tray of loose parts: these ask nothing and offer everything. The child decides what it becomes.

We would rather carry fewer things and stand behind them completely. Every item in this catalog is one we have stood behind: to educators, to parents, to school procurement officers who need to justify a budget line.

And we believe the best classrooms smell like wood, feel like wool, and sound like children absorbed in play.

What Schools Can Expect

Purchase orders accepted. Tax-exempt orders supported. Every catalog item is selected for durability, open-ended use, and meaningful classroom play. Need help building a shelf, center, or full classroom set? Send us your age range, budget, or curriculum goal and we’ll help assemble a thoughtful order.

The Procurement Notes

The fine print, plainly stated.

Answers to the questions schools tend to ask after a quote has been issued and before a purchase order has been signed.

Stock confirmed on every quote.

Every quote confirms current inventory on every line item. If anything is short, we flag it before you submit a PO.

Pricing held for 30 days.

Quoted pricing is honored for 30 calendar days from quote date. Plenty of time for district approval and PO issuance.

If something goes out of stock.

If a quoted item becomes unavailable between quote and PO, we substitute with your written approval or remove it from the order.

Vendor forms, one business day.

Send your district’s vendor onboarding form to accounting@mytoywagon.com and we return it completed within one business day.

Ship to multiple classrooms.

Drop-ship to multiple classroom addresses on one PO. We do this regularly. Email educators@mytoywagon.com for a coordinated quote.

Sole-source letters, on request.

For districts that require a non-competitive-bid justification (Tara Treasures, Bauspiel, and other genuinely unique brands), we write the letter within two business days.

Tax-exempt, all 50 states.

We accept tax-exempt certificates from any state. Once on file, sales tax is waived on every future invoice automatically.

Sustainability, on the record.

Tara Treasures is Fair Trade USA Certified. Bauspiel and Drewart use FSC-certified European hardwood. Most of our line is OEKO-TEX or GOTS certified.

Cancellations after fulfillment.

A PO cancelled after pick-and-pack has begun is subject to a 15% restocking fee plus return shipping. Cancellations before fulfillment are free.

Payment terms.

Net-30 is our default. Net-60 available for larger or recurring orders. Net-60 is our maximum; extensions are reviewed case by case.
