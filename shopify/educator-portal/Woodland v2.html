Essay One  ·  What the Woodland Holds

A family of small folk, a forest floor that holds them.

The woodland is the world a young child knows by instinct: a mossy place where small folk live in burrows, gather around mushrooms, and tend to one another through a long, slow season. Our Woodland Collection is hand-felted gnome families, Waldorf folk dolls, and the wool understory that holds them. No batteries. No instructions. No right way. A nature table becomes a forest floor, and the forest floor becomes the story.
